# Product Inquiry — Example Emails & Responses
Last updated: 2026-03-23

Reference examples for drafting responses to customer product inquiries.
Claude uses these to match YPS's voice, tone, and response patterns.

---

## Key Patterns

1. **Always steer to part number matching** — never rely on VIN, vehicle specs, or eBay fitment
2. **Guide the customer** — tell them where to find the part number (stickers, scan tool, photos)
3. **Offer to help identify from photos** — "send us pictures and we'll point you in the right direction"
4. **VIN + mileage at checkout** — for programmable units, customer enters in "Add note to seller" box; still need part number first

---

## Example 1: ECU — Customer Has Hardware Number Instead of Software Number

**Customer email:**
- **Subject:** [ECU inquiry]
- **Body:**
> so dealer said part number is 68192083AH

**YPS response:**
> That is a hardware number. You need to get the software number by scanning your ecu or checking the part numbers. Thanks! YPS

**Notes:**
- Short and direct — corrects the misunderstanding without being condescending
- Points them to the next step (scan tool or check part numbers)

## Example 2: ECU — Customer Provides Mopar SKU from VIN Lookup

**Customer email:**
- **Subject:** [ECU inquiry]
- **Body:**
> so by Mopar website and vin this is the sku for the pcm 5150790AC

**YPS response:**
> (Continued conversation — guided customer to locate stickers on the ECU)

**Customer follow-up:**
> are they on the back or sides or front

**YPS response:**
> They will be on the opposite side of the connectors. Thanks, YPS

**Then:**
> Hello, you need to match your part number. Your ecu will have a sticker or two on it with some numbers. If you wanted to send us pictures of those stickers we can point you in the right direction. Thanks, YPS

**Notes:**
- Patient multi-message exchange guiding the customer step by step
- Offers to identify from photos — low friction for the customer
- Ignores eBay's AI-generated response suggestion

## Example 3: Rebuilt Module — Customer Asks About VIN/Mileage Pre-Programming

**Customer email:**
- **Subject:** [rebuilt module inquiry]
- **Body:**
> Do i need to send you the vin.. and mileage before you ship it so all I have to do is install it?? Is that how you do it

**YPS response:**
> Hello and thanks for your interest in our rebuilt module.
> Yes we would need the VIN and mileage before it ships. But you also need to match your part number.
> DO NOT RELY ON EBAY'S FITMENT GUIDE OR THE GUARANTEED TO FIT PROGRAM!
> These modules are part number or family specific. The best way to move forward is to get your part number. These are located under the driver door under the vehicle. If you crawl under the vehicle under the driver door and look up over where the brake lines go into the pump you normally can see the front of the module where the part number sticker is located. Once you have located that let us know the part number and we'll point you in the right direction.
> Please email with any questions. Thanks again!
> YPS

**Notes:**
- Confirms yes to VIN/mileage but immediately pivots to part number matching
- Gives specific physical location instructions for finding the part number
- Includes the fitment guide warning
- Warm greeting and sign-off

## Example 4: VIN Compatibility — Customer Asks If VIN Can Determine Fit

**Customer email:**
- **Subject:** [compatibility inquiry]
- **Body:**
> If I send you the VIN number, can you tell me if it's compatible?

**YPS response:**
> Hello and thank you for your interest.
> DO NOT RELY ON EBAY'S FITMENT GUIDE OR THE GUARANTEED TO FIT PROGRAM!
> Over 33% of our buyers do not read this and buy the wrong part number!!!
> These units are part number specific and not vehicle specific. We do not have VIN interchange and recommend matching your current part number to ensure you don't have any fitment issues. When you have a moment if you could please let us know what your current part number is we will check our inventory to see if we have a unit which will work for your vehicle.
> We will await your reply. Please email with any questions. Thanks again!
> YPS

**Notes:**
- Directly addresses the VIN question — "We do not have VIN interchange"
- Explains WHY part number matters (33% wrong part stat)
- Asks them to provide the part number so YPS can check inventory
- Professional but firm

## Example 5: Multifunction Module — Customer Provides Vehicle Info Instead of Part Number

**Customer email:**
- **Subject:** [multifunction module inquiry]
- **Body:**
> Hello. Wondering if this multifunction module matches mine.
> It's a 2002 King Ranch Crew Cab 5.4 Lt Has Naturally Aspirated.

**YPS response:**
> Hello and thank you for your interest.
> DO NOT RELY ON EBAY'S FITMENT GUIDE OR THE GUARANTEED TO FIT PROGRAM!
> Over 33% of our buyers do not read this and buy the wrong part number!!!
> These units are part number specific and not vehicle specific. We do not have VIN interchange and recommend matching your current part number to ensure you don't have any fitment issues. It looks like your part number sticker may be missing or was not visible in the pictures. You may need to use a scan tool to get it. When you have a moment if you could please let us know what your current part number is we will check our inventory to see if we have a unit which will work for your vehicle.
> We will await your reply. Please email with any questions. Thanks again!
> YPS

**Notes:**
- Same core message as Example 4 but adapted — mentions missing sticker and scan tool option
- Doesn't try to answer based on vehicle specs alone
- Consistent structure and tone across similar inquiries

## Example 6: Fitment Question — Customer Assumes Year = Compatible

**Customer email:**
- **Subject:** [Dodge Viper ECU inquiry]
- **Body:**
> have a 2005 dodge viper. Should fit just fine? I assume all of these are the same for the year, there weren't any engine configuration differences or body style changes

**YPS response:**
> You'll want to locate your part number. We aren't sure Dodge ever made just one part # for a vehicle in any one year. If unsure you can shoot us a picture of any sticker on the unit and we'll do what we can to point you in the right direction. We also have large zip ties we can send with the unit to help secure that connector in place. Thanks again! YPS

**Notes:**
- Gently corrects the assumption without being dismissive
- Offers photo identification as an easy alternative
- Adds a helpful extra (zip ties for connector) — shows product knowledge and care
- More conversational tone for a more casual customer
