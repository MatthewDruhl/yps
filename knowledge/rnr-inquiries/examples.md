# R&R Inquiry — Example Emails & Responses
Last updated: 2026-03-27

Reference examples for drafting responses to customer repair and return inquiries.
Claude uses these to match YPS's voice, tone, and response patterns.

---

## Key Patterns

1. **Collect full diagnostic info before committing** — not all units are repairable
2. **Always disclose the diagnostic fee upfront** — use the closing paragraph from rnr-info.md
3. **Never ask for VIN or mileage** — not needed for R&R
4. **Check for disqualifying conditions first** — P06xx codes, ineligible product types, ineligible makes/years
5. **Friendly, detailed tone** — customers are trusting YPS with their broken unit

---

## Example 1: Ford PCM R&R — Incomplete Code Set (P0443 Pattern)

**Customer email:**
- **Subject:** REPAIR SERVICE 05-10 F-250 F-350 F-450 6.8L ECU ECM PCM ENGINE COMPUTER P0443
- **Body:**
> hello... looks like my truck might be a good candidate for PCM reflash. Can you confirm. See details attached. All 3 codes come up pretty quickly each time i restart the truck after clearing them.
>
> Vehicle: 2007 Ford F-350 Triton
>
> Codes: P0135 — O2 Sensor Heater 1, P0155 — O2 Sensor Heater 2, P0443 — EVAP Canister Solenoid
>
> Online, there were various suggestions that it was a blown fuse shared by those 3 components... but all of them seemed to be false leads (the fuses didn't exist). Your opinion appreciated before I begin the process of extracting the PCM and sending in etc.

**YPS response:**
> Hello, thanks for reaching out and for the detailed information!
>
> We do offer a repair and return service for some Ford PCMs. To quickly determine if we can help, we'd like to get a bit more information from you:
>
> 1. Are there any additional trouble codes set in the PCM? Usually when the PCM is the issue we will see a few additional trouble codes than what you have.
> 2. Part number on your PCM — Ford PCMs have a sticker on the unit with "12A650" in the middle, that sticker contains your part number.
> 3. Engine size — can you confirm the engine on your F-350?
> 4. What diagnostics were completed that led to the PCM as the suspected issue?
> 5. Any other parts already tested, replaced, or repaired?
>
> Unfortunately not all of these units are repairable. This is the reason we ask for so much information up front as we do have a diagnostic fee of $94.99 that we would refund you down to if the unit comes in and either tests good or winds up being unrepairable. We do not want you to send it to us unless we have a good shot at a successful repair.
>
> We will await your reply and have our techs review the information. Let us know if you have any questions — we are here to help!
>
> YPS

**Notes:**
- Customer used "reflash" — we don't correct the terminology, we just describe what we offer (repair and return)
- Customer only had P0135, P0155, P0443 — missing P0403, P0141, P0161. When the PCM is the root cause of this pattern we expect all 6 codes. Ask neutrally without explaining where the codes point.
- Always disclose the $94.99 diagnostic fee upfront in R&R replies — include the full closing paragraph from rnr-info.md
- Do not include "external to the PCM" logic in the reply
